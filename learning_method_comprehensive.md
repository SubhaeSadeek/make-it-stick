# Comprehensive Learning Method:

## Forgetting is not a flaw. It's a function.

- **Forgetting is normal**: The brain discards information that isn't reinforced. Traditional study methods don't work because they don't challenge the brain to actively recall information.

- **Two essential phases:** 
	
	1) Accurate acquisition
		- Making sure what enters your brain is clear and meaningful.
	
	2) Durable retention
		- Making sure what enters-stays.

- **MAPS Framework: This framework is used to encode knowledge deeply:**
    - Methods: Learning by doing. 
		- Put the new knowledge into use, right away.
		- Explain what you've just read in real time.
	
    - Analogies: Creating mental connections.
		- The more links you create between new and old knowledge, the easier recall becomes.
		- The more vivid and familiar the connection, the stronger the memory.
	
    - Principles: Understanding the why.
	    - Why something works? (dig deeper into the concept you've just taken)
	
    - Specifics: Using active recall and spaced repetition.
	    1) Active recall: forcing your brain to retrieve information without looking at your notes.
	    2) Spaced repetition: reviewing the material in increasing intervals: one day, three days, one week. (Anky and Quizzlet)
		- The rule: "Struggle to remember, and the memory becomes stronger"


- **Retrieval Pyramid: The more you retrieve information, the stronger your memory becomes.**

# Merve's way to study

1) After studying a topic, I close the book and explain it in my own words.
2) Then, I use flashcards-question on one side, answer on the back- and test myself regularly.
3) I teach the concept to friend or simulate teaching it out loud, even if I'm alone.
4) I build mind maps to visualize the full topic-then recreate the map from memory.
