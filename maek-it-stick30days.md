# 30-Day _Make It Stick_ Workbook & Practice Planner

A structured month-long training system to convert passive studying into durable learning through retrieval, spacing, interleaving, elaboration, generation, and calibration.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS WORKBOOK

Each day contains:

- **Focus Principle** — the learning science target
- **Core Practice** — what you must do
- **Workbook Prompts** — written reflection / exercises
- **End-of-Day Calibration** — measure real retention, not time spent

**Required rule for all 30 days:**
You are forbidden to simply reread without retrieval.

Keep one notebook for all written responses.

---

# WEEK 1 — BREAKING THE PASSIVE STUDY HABIT

## Goal: Train the brain to retrieve instead of re-expose

### DAY 1 — Baseline Audit

**Focus Principle:** Awareness of false learning habits

Write down:

1. How do I usually study?
2. Which methods make me feel productive?
3. How much do I actually remember after 3 days?
4. What subjects/topics am I applying this workbook to?

**Core Practice:**
Study one short section of material. Close it. Write everything remembered.

**Calibration:**
How much vanished the moment the page closed?

---

### DAY 2 — First Retrieval Shock

**Focus Principle:** Retrieval > rereading

Study for 15 minutes only.
Then spend 15 minutes recalling from memory without looking.

**Workbook Prompts:**

- What could I recall clearly?
- What felt blurry?
- Did retrieval feel harder than rereading?

---

### DAY 3 — Question-Making Day

**Focus Principle:** Build prompts, not notes

Convert today’s material into at least 20 retrieval questions.
Examples:

- What are the 3 causes of X?
- Explain Y in simple words.
- Why does Z happen?

**Calibration:**
How many can I answer cold?

---

### DAY 4 — Delayed Recall Session

Return to Day 1 and Day 2 material without reviewing.
Attempt full recall first.
Then check.

**Reflection:**
Notice how forgetting creates effort.

---

### DAY 5 — Blank Page Reconstruction

Take a blank page.
Write all remembered concepts from this week’s learning.
No peeking.

Then mark:

- Solid
  n- Partial
- Missing

---

### DAY 6 — Explain Aloud Drill

Teach this week’s material aloud for 10–15 minutes.
If you stumble, write the exact gap.

---

### DAY 7 — Weekly Reality Check

Answer:

1. What study habit did I stop trusting?
2. What surprised me about retrieval?
3. Where is my confidence fake?

Conduct a 30-minute mixed self-test.

---

# WEEK 2 — SPACING AND DESIRABLE DIFFICULTY

## Goal: Learn to use forgetting as a tool

### DAY 8 — Build a Spaced Review Map

List all topics learned so far.
Schedule reviews for:

- tomorrow
- 3 days
- 7 days
- 14 days
- 30 days

---

### DAY 9 — Resist Immediate Peeking

For every forgotten answer, wait at least 60 seconds before checking.
Write how often your brain retrieved after struggle.

---

### DAY 10 — Hard Recall Repetition

Do 3 rounds:

- retrieve
- check
- close
- retrieve again

Notice second recall improvement.

---

### DAY 11 — Rustiness Acceptance Day

Review older material that feels forgotten.
Document this:
"What returned after effort?"

---

### DAY 12 — Deliberate Difficulty

Use harder prompts:

- compare concepts
  n- explain causes
- generate examples
- teach without keywords

---

### DAY 13 — Delayed Self-Quiz

No studying first.
Quiz before touching notes.
Then fill gaps.

---

### DAY 14 — Weekly Review + Reflection

What feels mentally uncomfortable now that did not before?
Which discomfort is productive?

Complete a 45-minute spaced recall session.

---

# WEEK 3 — INTERLEAVING, GENERATION, ELABORATION

## Goal: Build flexible understanding instead of chapter familiarity

### DAY 15 — Mix Subjects

Study three topics in rotating 20-minute blocks.
No long single-topic binge.

Reflect on mental switching difficulty.

---

### DAY 16 — Mixed Problem Solving

Do not group same-type tasks.
Mix question types intentionally.

Write: How did this force recognition?

---

### DAY 17 — Guess Before Seeing

Before reading any answer/explanation, predict it.
Record:

- My guess
- Actual answer
- Why I missed

---

### DAY 18 — Failure as Encoding

Choose 10 difficult questions.
Attempt all cold.
Celebrate wrong answers as memory hooks.
Analyze each mistake.

---

### DAY 19 — Elaboration Chain

For each major concept ask:

- Why?
- How?
- Example?
- Opposite?
- Real-life use?

Do this for at least 5 concepts.

---

### DAY 20 — Connection Web

Draw a map connecting this month’s topics.
How does one idea lead to another?

---

### DAY 21 — Weekly Mixed Master Test

One hour.
No notes.
Mixed topics.
Short answer, explanation, recall, application.

Then write:
Where am I still pattern-matching instead of understanding?

---

# WEEK 4 — CONSOLIDATION INTO A LIFELONG SYSTEM

## Goal: Turn these principles into permanent habit

### DAY 22 — Personal Retrieval Routine Design

Design your ideal daily retrieval session:

- new learning
- old review
- question prompts
- explanation

---

### DAY 23 — Confidence vs Competence Audit

For each topic score:

- How confident do I feel?
- How much can I actually retrieve?

Notice mismatches.

---

### DAY 24 — Teach to an Imaginary Beginner

Explain a difficult topic in plain language.
No jargon allowed.

---

### DAY 25 — Speed Recall Drill

Rapid-fire answer 30 prompts.
Mark instant / delayed / failed.

---

### DAY 26 — Deep Gap Repair

Choose weakest 3 areas.
Do targeted retrieval loops until improved.

---

### DAY 27 — Interleaved Marathon

90-minute mixed rotation:
old + new + weak + application.

Document mental stamina.

---

### DAY 28 — Independent Master Summary

Write a complete summary of the month’s learned material entirely from memory.
Then compare with sources.

---

### DAY 29 — Build Your Long-Term Spacing Calendar

Create a future 8-week review schedule.
Decide exact days and topics.

---

### DAY 30 — Final Transformation Review

Answer in depth:

1. How has my concept of studying changed?
2. Which old habits now seem useless?
3. What retrieval system will I continue?
4. What evidence shows my learning is stickier?
5. What is my personal Make It Stick manifesto?

Finish with a 60-minute comprehensive self-test.
